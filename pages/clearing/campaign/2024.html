Biology clearing places

Clearing is now open. We're here to help you get ready for results day and find your place in one of our Biology courses, with places available for September 2024. 

Sign up for Clearing priority email updates and benefit from exclusive support including: 

  • live Q&A sessions,
  • campus tours,
  • access to live chat with our clearing advisers, and
  • email updates with tips, clearing advice and access to our clearing course list.

Find your Biology place at the University of Stirling

Are you interested in the evolution of animal behaviour, the genetic engineering of crops or the hidden worlds of molecular and cell biology? Are you curious about how plants and animals will respond to climate change? Our Biology courses will give you the skills and knowledge needed for a wide range of diverse careers in biology.

Our course will teach you the full scope of life through multiple biological disciplines: from bacteria to blue whales, from enzymes to ecosystems. As you develop your practical skills you'll become involved with research, giving you the opportunity to contribute to biological discoveries.

We're ranked Top 5 in Scotland for Student Satisfaction in Biology (Guardian University Guide, 2023).

  • BSc (Hons) Animal Biology
  • BSc (Hons) Cell Biology
  • BSc (Hons) Marine Biology

We also have Clearing spaces available in a wide range of subjects: from Criminology to Computing; French to Finance; Sociology to Science; and many more.

Year 4 tuition fees: Covered

Students from England, Wales, Northern Ireland and Republic of Ireland may be eligible for £9,250 scholarships covering your Honours year tuition fees (terms and conditions apply). Find out about these and other scholarships.

Guaranteed accommodation

Apply by 31 August and you'll be guaranteed an offer of University-managed accommodation in your first year of study (terms and conditions apply).

Support when you need it

We offer an extensive range of support services to help you fulfil your potential and make the most of your time at university. You can get advice about mental health and wellbeing, money issues, careers and much more.

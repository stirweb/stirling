Careers Service

The Careers and Employability Service has a wealth of resources that are valuable not only to students, but to all staff and researchers.  Read on to discover how we can support you!

Careers and Employability Staff Resource Hub

This SharePoint site supports staff to embed employability in the curriculum.  It provides resources, case studies, and examples of good practice being delivered throughout the university.

Join the Staff Resource Hub to discover how we

  • network with employers and bring them to meet with students and staff (remotely or on-campus)
  • support placements
  • run the My Stirling Award
  • contribute to wider University of Stirling initiatives and pilots such as Graduate Apprenticeships, Inplace and the Riipen work-based learning platform
  • develop policy (Work Placement and Project Policy WPPP)
  • engage in the Enhancement Themes
  • co-ordinate the Graduate Outcomes survey
  • collect and analyse data relevant to employability such as the exit survey and the career readiness survey conducted annually
  • provide staff development

Introduction to Careers and Employability Service

Other resources for staff

1. Careers Service Canvas site

We recommend you join the Careers on Canvas site if, as a staff member, you require any career advice.  There are over 300 pages of information on job hunting, occupations, application advice, and much more.

Careers on Canvas also has a dedicated section providing advice for Postgraduate researchers and research staff.  All our Careers Consultants have experience working with researchers and we offer one-to-one appointments every day; these can be booked via TARGETconnect. We also host several opportunities to engage with employers and organisations that want to recruit students, including researchers, and we host insight Panels of professionals working in specific industries – you can see all our upcoming events and register a space on TARGETconnect. We work closely with our colleagues in the Institute for Advanced Studies to contribute to a programme of support, and regularly run workshops including topics such as Career Options outside of Academia, and CVs for Researchers.

2. University external website

The Careers and Employability Service employer pages on the University website are a useful resource if you are in contact with an employer who is interested in working with the University and our students.  They may have a job opportunity they want to advertise, be able to support a student on placement, or wish to be involved in a careers event.  Directing the employer to these web pages will allow them to know what support we can provide and how to contact us.

3. TARGETconnect

Staff can join our online platform TARGETconnect where vacancies, volunteering opportunities and career events are advertised. If you have a job, volunteering or paid internship opportunity that would be suitable for a student or graduate please contact employers@stir.ac.uk and request to be added as a contact for the University employer’s account where you can log in and post your opportunity.

Please note that paid internships can run part-time during the semester (15 hours a week maximum) or part-time or full-time during the summer vacation (4 to 12 weeks). Internship projects could include a piece of research, social media projects, database design and event organisation. Please contact us with any other ideas you may have by emailing internship@stir.ac.uk.

You can help us support students with their career planning by encouraging them to

  1.  Enrol with the Careers and Employability Service on Canvas to access a wealth of information on exploring their options, job hunting, CVs and applications, interviews, postgraduate study and lots more.
  2. Register for the My Stirling Award to gain recognition for their extra-curricular achievements. Split into three levels - Bronze, Silver and Gold. Part-time work, volunteering, attending events and involvement with university sports clubs and societies all count.
  3. Activate TARGETconnect to find work and volunteering opportunities, register for upcoming events or workshops, and make an appointment with a Careers and Employability Consultant
  4. Score and improve their CV using CareerSet, an online platform that scans CVs against top recruiter and hiring managers criteria.  Cover letters can also be enhanced using core checks and relevancy checks.
